Background

Finding summer storage can be a real issue for students at Cornell. With a hectic finals schedule, limited availabilities, and inflexible spending power, students have to face the current storage options that are expensive with immovable schedules. Ezrabox aims to solve all the issues for summer storage faced by a Cornell student.

We are a peer-to-peer storage platform that connects students who need space to store their items over summer with students who have space and capacity to store othersâ€™ items.

Our History

Founded in 2016, Ezrabox initially started as an advocacy initiative within the Cornell International Students Union. Over time, Ezrabox has become its own entity and went through the Cornell accelerator program (eLab) to become an independent student-led startup at Cornell.

During the spring semester of 2018, Ezrabox had its first official operational cycle. The result of the business cycle included 650+ registrations on our platform, 150+ paid transactions, $10,000+ in total revenue and an estimated +$12,000 saved for our customers. Although the cycle was fairly successful for our first official cycle, we know we can improve.
