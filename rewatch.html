‣ Caffeine
In this project, I designed the end-to-end coffee caffeine tracker app for Apple Watch. After research I found that people find it difficult to break bad coffee consumption habits. I designed a solution that provides users healthy alternatives to disencourage excess caffeine consumption.
Timeline: Fall 2020, Side project
Contributors: Soyee
Background
High-level Problem: In a study funded by the National Institute on Drug Abuse, 44% of people with caffeine dependence continued consumption despite harm and only 23% expressed desire to cut down or control use.
Risk of excess caffiene intake can lead to the following health concerns: Liver damage, Increase blood pressure, head ache, anxiety, insomnia and more.
Source: Journal of Caffeine Research: Evaluating Dependence Criteria for Caffeine
Research
User Research
I interviewed people who drink Coffee on a day to day basis understand their pain-points and discover the problem. My key insights are the following:
  1. People are unaware of the recommended caffeine intake
  2. People don't feel the need to stop drinking coffee until they experience a health problem
  3. People who drink too much coffee drink it as a habit
Competitive analysis
I took a look at other caffeine tracking apps to identify strengths and weaknesses on how they are solving the problem.
Define
Problem statement: People want to drink coffee without health risks but they can't do that because they are unaware of their recommended caffeine intake and are unmotivated to break consumption habits
Target Audience: Coffee lovers who care about their health and wellness but struggle because of excess caffeine consumption.
Ideation
Brain storming: How might we help users limit excess caffeine intake?
Problem Solution: Caffeine tracker app that suggests other healthy alternatives when consumed over the targeted limit. Wearable tech brings convenience, real-time tracking, and efficiency.
