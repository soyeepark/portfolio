Caffeine

Product Designer / Fall 2020

Overview

Contributors: Soyee Park | Tools: Figma, Sketch, InVision
In this project, I designed the end-to-end coffee caffeine tracker app for Apple Watch. After research I found that people find it difficult to break bad coffee consumption habits. I designed a solution that provides users with healthy alternatives to disencourage excess caffeine consumption.

Research

In a study funded by the National Institute on Drug Abuse, 44% of people with caffeine dependence continued consumption despite harm and only 23% expressed desire to cut down or control use.
Many studies prove that excess caffeine intake can lead to health concerns such as liver damage, increased blood pressure, headache, anxiety, insomnia and more.
Source: Journal of Caffeine Research: Evaluating Dependence Criteria for Caffeine
After learning about these health concerns, I conducted qualitative interviews with people who drink Coffee on a day to day basis to understand their pain-points and nail down the problem space. My key insights were the following:
  • People are unaware of the recommended caffeine intake.
  • People don't feel the need to stop drinking coffee until they experience a stomach ache.
  • People who drink too much coffee drink it as a habit.

Define

Problem statement: People want to drink coffee while maintaining health but they are unaware of their recommended caffeine intake and don’t know how to break unhealthy consumption habits
Target Audience: Coffee lovers who care about their health and wellness but struggle because of excess caffeine consumption.
Actionable insights:
  • How might we make users aware of their recommended caffeine intake?
  • How might we alert users in advance and prevent them from health risks from too much caffeine?
  • How might we help users break unhealthy caffeine consumption habits?

Ideation

I created an impact-feasibility matrix to brainstorm the optimal solution to the design challenge. I facilitated a brainstorming workshop with my friends and we synthesized our ideas into 6 buckets of ideas.
After the brainstorming session, we chose to iterate on the idea “suggest other healthy alternatives”. We thought it was the most feasible and high impact idea because we foresaw long-term benefits from users picking up healthier alternatives.
During research, participants mentioned the importance of convenience, real-time tracking, and efficiency. When considering these user needs, I found that wearable tech could amplify the end-to-end experience as it leverages these benefits. For this case study, I chose to design specifically for an Apple Watch.

Competitive analysis

Before jumping into low fidelity designs, I wanted to understand how other Apple watch caffeine apps position their features and product. I looked at their Apple watch app but also referenced their mobile app as well.
Similar apps did not provide users with alternate suggestions. Instead, they were more of a journaling tool for caffeine intake. Through my audit, I learned about the following patterns as well:
  • Home screen provides a record of a user’s caffeine intake at a glance. Weekly insights are hidden to prevent overload on the homescreen.
  • Provide customization to be able to add drinks accurately and easily. Less taps and exposed/clear entry point.
  • Data visualizations are used to provide digestible information with visuals.
In addition, I learned about patterns used specifically for Apple watch apps to understand how to design for its device:
  • Interactions are fast and efficient. Each end-to-end interaction takes around 3 taps or less. Prevent long scrolls.
  • Use of simple visualizations that are easy to understand. Prevent overkill of complex visuals -- sometimes numbers and text can do just enough.
  • Prevent use of too much color to avoid adding excess noise.

Final thoughts

  • Hardest challenges: Most watch activities are designed to encourage a behavior. It was challenging to design a visualization to limit or discourage consumption.
  • What I learned: How to design on a small screen real-estate and prioritize fast selection/efficient user flow
  • Moving forward: I want to work on prototyping dynamic micro interactions.
