Kajak

Product Designer / Winter 2020

Overview

Kajak is a mobile-first ask and answer platform where consumers get access to continuously relevant, quality content, and creators get paid for their unique knowledge and skills. I was the founding designer on both platforms (Android and iOS).

Problem

One of the most popular topics of exchange are beauty & fashion, health & wellness, finance, parenting, and language & career. In Indonesia, a main channel of information exchange are through groupchats such as WhatsApp, Line and Facebook groups. However, it is difficult to find reliable, trustable and upto-date information because:

  • In group chats, topics are not structured and comments are disjointed on incumbent platforms.

  • Indonesia lacks content to populate, but because creators lack incentives to tell the truth because they make money off of clicks.

  • Key opinion leaders face unstable income streams and friction to monetizing their most ardent fans because the advertising market is not robust in Southeast Asia.

  • There is widespread misinformation and noise on existing platforms like Facebook and Instagram.

Solution

Kajak helps key opinion leaders (KOLs) create and operate high-quality communities. Opinion leaders create “planets,” which are small groups for exclusive content and discussion. Fans pay a fixed fee to gain membership to “planets” for a certain amount of time.

Key opinion leaders use Kajak to:

  • Create a planet to manage their fans

  • Share educational tutorials through articles, photos, files, and URLs

  • Tag posts with topics to organize information

  • Watermark planets to prevent screenshots

The primary value for Fans is access to:

  • Ask questions to opinion leaders

  • Leave comments on posts and reply to members

  • Meet and build relationships with like-minded fans

  • Find any information by search keywords

Learnings

This was my first time designing for an app for both iOS and Android. Although detailed specs can help engineers build out the app, I learned that the best implementation happens when you are best friends with your engineers. If your engineers care about design, they can bring up interesting solutions and questions that make your designs better as well.
